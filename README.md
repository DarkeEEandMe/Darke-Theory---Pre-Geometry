Darke Theory - Structural Cosmology and Pre-Geometry Research

This repository contains the core mathematical framework and applied research papers of the Darke Theory programme, a structural approach to cosmology and fundamental physics.

The work investigates the possibility that spacetime geometry is not fundamental, but instead emerges from a deeper process of directed relational refinement and stabilised closure.

The programme begins from a minimal assumption:

difference.

No spacetime manifold, quantum fields, particles, or matter content are assumed at the starting point.

From this minimal premise, a structured dynamical framework is developed in which relational distinguishability evolves through Stable Directed Refinement Systems (SDRS).

Structural Development:

Within this framework a refinement flow operates on relational structure.
Through stability constraints and finite closure capacity the following structures emerge naturally:

• finite generator sectors
• Lie-algebraic structure of refinement operators
• invariant quadratic forms
• emergent complex structure and unitary evolution
• uncertainty relations arising from non-commutativity
• conditional deformation rigidity
• classification of stable directed refinement systems

In this view geometry is not primitive.
Instead it appears as a representation of coherence relations within stabilised structure.

Spacetime curvature, gravitational wells, and cosmological dynamics arise as macroscopic manifestations of this underlying refinement process.

Research Objective:

The broader goal of the Darke Theory programme is to investigate whether a coherence-first description of physical law can provide a deeper explanation for:

• spacetime geometry
• gravitational curvature
• quantum structure
• early-universe cosmology
• compact astrophysical phenomena

Rather than treating geometry as fundamental, this work explores the hypothesis that geometry emerges from stabilised relational structure.
